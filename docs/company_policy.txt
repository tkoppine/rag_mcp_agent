ACME CORPORATION — EMPLOYEE POLICY HANDBOOK
Version 3.2 | Effective Date: January 1, 2026
============================================================

1. CODE OF CONDUCT
------------------------------------------------------------
All employees are expected to maintain a professional and respectful work environment. This includes:

- Treating all colleagues, clients, and partners with dignity and respect regardless of race, gender, age, religion, disability, or sexual orientation.
- Avoiding conflicts of interest. Employees must disclose any personal or financial relationships with vendors, clients, or competitors to their manager and HR within 30 days of the relationship forming.
- Maintaining confidentiality of proprietary company information, trade secrets, and client data at all times, including after employment ends.
- Zero tolerance for harassment, bullying, or discriminatory behavior. Violations may result in immediate termination.


2. WORK HOURS AND ATTENDANCE
------------------------------------------------------------
Standard working hours are Monday through Friday, 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM local time, with a one-hour unpaid lunch break.

- Flexible work hours: Employees may shift their schedule by up to two hours (e.g., 8 AM–5 PM or 10 AM–7 PM) with manager approval.
- Remote work: Employees in eligible roles may work remotely up to 3 days per week. Full-remote arrangements require VP-level approval and a signed remote work agreement.
- Attendance: Employees must notify their manager and update their calendar before 9:30 AM on any day they will be absent or significantly late.
- Overtime: Non-exempt employees working more than 40 hours per week will be compensated at 1.5x their hourly rate. Overtime must be pre-approved by a manager.


3. LEAVE POLICY
------------------------------------------------------------
3.1 Paid Time Off (PTO)
Employees accrue PTO based on years of service:
  - 0–2 years:   15 days per year
  - 3–5 years:   20 days per year
  - 6–10 years:  25 days per year
  - 10+ years:   30 days per year

PTO must be requested at least 5 business days in advance for durations of 3 or more days. Unused PTO up to 10 days may be carried over to the next calendar year. Any excess above 10 days is forfeited at year-end.

3.2 Sick Leave
Employees receive 10 days of paid sick leave per year. Sick leave does not carry over. A doctor's note is required for absences exceeding 3 consecutive days.

3.3 Parental Leave
Primary caregivers receive 16 weeks of fully paid parental leave. Secondary caregivers receive 4 weeks of fully paid parental leave. Leave must be taken within 12 months of the birth, adoption, or foster placement of a child.

3.4 Bereavement Leave
Employees are entitled to up to 5 days of paid bereavement leave for the death of an immediate family member (spouse, child, parent, sibling) and 3 days for extended family (grandparents, in-laws, aunts, uncles).


4. EXPENSE REIMBURSEMENT
------------------------------------------------------------
The company reimburses reasonable and necessary business expenses incurred while performing job duties.

- All expense claims must be submitted via the Concur expense portal within 30 days of the expense being incurred.
- Receipts are required for any single expense over $25.
- Meals: Up to $75 per person for client entertainment meals; up to $40 per person for internal team meals. Alcohol may not exceed 20% of a meal receipt.
- Travel: Economy class for flights under 6 hours; business class permitted for flights over 6 hours with manager approval. Hotel bookings should not exceed the city's published GSA per diem rate by more than 25%.
- Personal expenses such as gym memberships, personal subscriptions, and non-business travel are not reimbursable.


5. DATA SECURITY AND ACCEPTABLE USE
------------------------------------------------------------
Employees are responsible for protecting company data and using company systems appropriately.

- All devices used for company work must have full-disk encryption enabled and be protected by a strong password or biometric lock.
- Employees must not install unauthorized software on company devices. All software installations must go through IT via the ServiceNow portal.
- Sensitive data (customer PII, financial records, source code) must not be stored on personal devices, personal cloud accounts, or unencrypted USB drives.
- Sharing company credentials with anyone, including other employees, is strictly prohibited. Each employee is responsible for all activity performed under their credentials.
- Employees who suspect a security breach or phishing attempt must report it immediately to security@acme.com or via the #security-alerts Slack channel.


6. PERFORMANCE REVIEWS
------------------------------------------------------------
ACME conducts formal performance reviews twice per year:

- Mid-Year Check-In (June): Informal review focused on goal progress, feedback, and course correction.
- Annual Review (December): Formal evaluation used to determine merit increases, bonuses, and promotion eligibility.

Employees are evaluated on: delivery of goals, quality of work, collaboration, communication, and leadership (for senior roles). Ratings are: Exceeds Expectations, Meets Expectations, Partially Meets Expectations, and Does Not Meet Expectations. Employees rated "Does Not Meet Expectations" twice consecutively may be placed on a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP).


7. LEARNING AND DEVELOPMENT
------------------------------------------------------------
ACME is committed to employee growth.

- Annual learning budget: Each full-time employee receives $2,000 per year to spend on approved courses, conferences, books, or certifications. Unused budget does not roll over.
- Internal training: Employees are encouraged to attend monthly internal tech talks and quarterly workshops organized by the L&D team.
- Tuition reimbursement: Employees pursuing job-relevant degrees may apply for up to $5,000 per year in tuition reimbursement. Requires manager and HR approval, and a minimum grade of B.


8. HEALTH AND WELLNESS BENEFITS
------------------------------------------------------------
- Medical: ACME covers 90% of employee premiums and 70% of dependent premiums for all medical, dental, and vision plans.
- 401(k): Company matches 100% of employee contributions up to 4% of annual salary. Employees are vested after 2 years.
- Mental health: All employees have access to 12 free counseling sessions per year through the Employee Assistance Program (EAP). Contact eap@acme.com for details.
- Wellness stipend: Employees receive a $500 annual wellness stipend for gym memberships, fitness equipment, or wellness apps.


9. DISCIPLINARY PROCESS
------------------------------------------------------------
Policy violations are addressed through a progressive disciplinary process:

  Step 1 — Verbal Warning: Manager addresses the issue informally and documents the conversation in the HR system.
  Step 2 — Written Warning: Formal written notice outlining the violation, expected behavior, and consequences of non-compliance.
  Step 3 — Performance Improvement Plan (PIP): A 30–90 day structured plan with specific measurable goals. Failure to meet PIP goals may result in termination.
  Step 4 — Termination: Employment ends if issues persist or the violation is severe enough to warrant immediate dismissal.

Certain violations (fraud, harassment, violence, data theft) may result in immediate termination without prior warnings.
